What is a Bone Marrow Transplant?

A bone marrow transplant is a medical procedure that replaces damaged or destroyed bone marrow in a patient with healthy bone marrow from a donor. The procedure involves transplanting blood stem cells, which then travel to the bone marrow, and subsequently promote the growth of new marrow.

Bone marrow is the spongy, fatty tissue that creates:

  • Red blood cells
  • White blood cells
  • Platelets

If the bone marrow cannot create the above-mentioned blood components due to disease, infection, radiation, or chemotherapy, a bone marrow transplant is needed.

Who Needs a Bone Marrow Transplant

Surya Hospital performs bone marrow transplants for a range of congenital and acquired disorders. We also specialize in peripheral stem cell and umbilical cord blood transplants to provide our patients with the highest level of care.
A bone marrow transplant is recommended to patients suffering from:

  • Benign Haematological Disorders
    • Thalassemia
    • Sickle Cell Disease
    • Aplastic Anemia
    • Bleeding Disorders
    • Coagulation Disorders, etc.
  • Malignant Haematological Disorders
    • Acute and Chronic Leukemia (AML/ALL/CML/CLL)
    • Hodgkins and Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma
    • Multiple Myeloma
    • Myelodysplasia (MDS), etc.
  • Acute and Chronic Blood Conditions
  • Inherited Immune Deficiency Syndromes
  • Metabolic Diseases
