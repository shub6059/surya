What is Clubfoot?

Clubfoot is a congenital deformity (present from birth) that is characterized by an inwardly curved or twisted foot and ankle. In clubfoot, the tendons and ligaments between muscles and bones are held on too tight, leading to an abnormal position of the foot. While the condition can be detected during an ultrasound before birth, the treatment only begins after birth. To increase the chances of an easy and successful treatment, it is recommended to start the treatment as soon as possible after birth.

What Are Treatment Options Avilable for Clubfoot

Casting
Casting is the method of choice for newborns and the Ponseti method is one of the most common techniques used for correcting clubfoot. In the Ponseti treatment method, our highly experienced paediatric orthopedic surgeon will gently stretch the baby’s foot to a relatively normal position and secure it with a cast. Within a course of 6-8 weeks, the foot is gradually stretched towards a normal position and the cast is replaced. This process is repeated 4-5 times depending on the improvement. Once the full correction is achieved, the child is braced for 2 years. The Ponseti method is a time-tested course of treatment which leads to no residual deformity in most cases. Casting is the preferred choice of treatment for babies who are under 2 weeks old. In cases where the clubfoot is too severe, casting may not work and surgery is needed for correction.

Surgery
Surgery is the preferred course of treatment for older children, adults, and in cases where casting doesn't lead to a successful correction. During surgery, our paediatric orthopedic surgeon lengthens your child’s Achilles tendon and releases the tissues in the foot. As a result, the ligaments and tendons loosen, allowing the surgeon to maneuver the foot into a normal position. The recovery process after the surgery involves casting for as long as 3 months in order to help the incisions, tendons, and bones to heal. The surgery is performed under general anaesthesia and is completely painless. To manage pain after the surgery, medication will be prescribed.
