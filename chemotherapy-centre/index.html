Frequently asked questions

Have questions? We're here to help.

What is chemotherapy?
  • Chemotherapy is any medicine or drug combination that kills, inhibits the growth of, or harms cancer cells.
How exactly is chemotherapy given?
  • There are various techniques to administer chemotherapy. Since many chemotherapy drugs are too potent to pass via the digestive system, they are administered intravenously. Chemotherapy administered intravenously (IV) is administered through a tube. The tube's other end is inserted into a vein, and one end is attached to a bag containing your medication.
    Some chemotherapy medications can be taken orally as pills or liquids, while others must be applied topically in the form of a cream or lotion. Chemotherapy can be administered intravenously.

    You can determine where the chemotherapy will be given by looking at the name of the injection.
Is chemotherapy painful?
  • Chemotherapy isn't painful for most patients. However, the side effects of chemotherapy drugs can be physically and emotionally challenging for some people that can be managed with guidance and therapies.
What is the duration of chemotherapy?
  • Chemotherapy is given throughout the course of several therapy sessions. The number of sessions you require will be determined by your cancer treatment team at the Dr. Advani Chemotherapy Therapy Center. A chemotherapy course is a comprehensive set of sessions.

    Your doctor normally lets you know when your chemotherapy will be finished when your treatments first start.
